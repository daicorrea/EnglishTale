<p>The words 'have to' describe something that needs to take place soon. </p>
<p>It expresses certainty, necessity, or obligation.</p>
<p></p>
<p>Here are some examples:</p>
<p></p>
<p>I have to switch schools.</p>
<p>I have to use the telephone.</p>
<p>I have to go to the bathroom.</p>
<p>I have to leave.</p>
<p>I have to unpack my bags.</p>
<p></p>
<p>You can also add the word 'don't' to suggest that someone is not required to do something.</p>
<p></p>
<p>I don't have to switch schools.</p>
<p>I don't have to use the telephone.</p>
<p>I don't have to go to the bathroom.</p>
<p>I don't have to leave.</p>
