<p>Definitions</p>
<p>A noun is a word or set of words for a person, place, thing, or idea.</p> 
<p>A noun of more than one word (tennis court, gas station) is called a compound noun.</p>
<p>There are common nouns and proper nouns.</p> 
<p>Common nouns are words for a general class of people, places, things, and ideas (man, city, award, honesty).</p> 
<p>They are not capitalized. Proper nouns are always capitalized. They name specific people, places, and things</p> 
<p>(Joe, Chicago, Academy Award).</p>
<p>A verb is a word or set of words that shows action (runs, is going, has been painting)</p> 
<p>feeling (loves, envies); or state of being (am, are, is, have been, was, seem).</p>
<p>Examples:</p>
<p>He ran around the block.</p> 
<p>I like my friend.</p>
<p>They seem friendly.</p>
<p>A subject is the noun, pronoun (see the "Pronouns" section later in this chapter),</p> 
<p>or set of words that performs the verb.</p>
<p>Examples:</p>
<p>The woman hurried.</p>
